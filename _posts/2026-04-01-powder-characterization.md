## Project Overview
------

Powder is the foundation of every metal AM build. Before a single layer is deposited, the quality, behavior, and properties of the feedstock powder are already determining the outcome — defect population, mechanical performance, and part-to-part consistency.

Yet powder characterization is one of the most fragmented topics in the field. Standards are scattered across ASTM, ISO, and joint ISO/ASTM committees. Methods range from simple gravity funnels to advanced powder rheometers. And the connection between a measured powder property and what actually happens inside the machine needs more exploration.

> You cannot optimize what you haven't characterized. And you cannot characterize effectively without knowing which test measures what — and why it matters."

This project maps the complete characterization framework for metallic powders used in Laser Powder Bed Fusion (L-PBF) and Electron Beam Powder Bed Fusion (EBM) — connecting characteristics, test methods, and standards into a single, navigable reference.

## About This Work
------

The objective of this project is to create a practical, decision-oriented reference that connects:

* Each powder characteristic — what it is, why it matters in AM, and how it influences process and part quality

* The test methods used to measure each characteristic — instrument, procedure, result format, and key limitations

* The applicable standards — ASTM and ISO references verified against the two primary AM powder anchor standards: ASTM F3049 and ISO/ASTM 52907

The map covers 6 primary characteristics:

* __Density__: Includes Apparent (Bulk), Tap, and True Density (Skeletal Density) densities and the primary test methods are Gas Pycnometry, Hall/Carney/Scott Apparatus, and Tap Density Tester.

* __Morphology__: Primary test methods are Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM), Static & Dynamic Image Analysis, and Sieving

* __Particle Size Distribution (PSD)__: Primary test methods are Laser Diffraction (Light Scattering), Sieve Analysis, Dynamic Image Analysis (DIA)

* __Flowability__: Primary test methods are Hall, Gustavsson & Carney Funnels, and FT4 Powder Rheometer (Advanced Rheometry)

* __Spreadability__: Primary test methods are In-Machine Evaluation, Spreading Testbeds, and Rotating Drum / Avalanche Angle.

* __Chemical Composition__: Primary test methods are ICP-OES, XRF, pXRF, LECO ONH & CS



## Scope & Prerequisites
------

This map focuses specifically on:

* Metallic powders for L-PBF and EB-PBF processes

* Characterization methods — how powder properties are measured and reported

* ASTM and ISO standards directly applicable to AM powder qualification

This is a live project — built to the best of my current knowledge and intended to evolve. There may be inaccuracies, and there is always room for improvement.

A note on scope: This map addresses characterization only — what the powder is and how to measure it. A companion project on Powder Defects & Flaws is planned as the next step, extending this framework upstream to the origins and failure modes of powder quality issues.

## What This Map Provides
------

The map presents a structured overview that links:

Characteristic → Test Method → Standard

The map is especially useful for:

* Process engineers designing powder qualification and incoming inspection protocols

* Materials engineers investigating powder reuse behavior and degradation

* Quality engineers building powder acceptance criteria aligned to current standards

* Anyone new to metal AM who needs a structured entry point into the powder characterization landscape
